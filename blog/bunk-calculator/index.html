How the bunk calculator works

The math is based on keeping your attendance ratio at or above your target g (for example 0.75 for 75%).

functions

Classes you can skip = floor( attended ÷ g − total )
Classes you must attend = ceil( (g × total − attended) ÷ (1 − g) )

The first formula assumes every future class you skip adds to the total but not to attended. The second finds how many classes you must attend in a row to climb back to target.

Skipping vs recovering

  • Above target? You have a buffer. The calculator shows how many upcoming classes you can miss before you hit the line.
  • Below target? There is no buffer — you need to attend a set number of classes consecutively to recover. Missing more digs the hole deeper.
lightbulb

Bunk with intent. Save your buffer for days you genuinely need — a clashing deadline, illness, or travel — rather than spending it early in the term.

Smart bunking tips

  • Track per subject, not just overall. Many colleges enforce attendance subject-by-subject, so one weak subject can bar you even if your average looks fine.
  • Recalculate after every week. Your buffer shrinks as more classes are held.
  • Never count cancelled classes in the total — they were never held.
  • Leave a safety margin. Aim a few points above the minimum so a surprise absence never pushes you under.

Key takeaways

  • A bunk calculator converts your attendance into a concrete "classes you can skip" number.
  • Above target you have a buffer; below target you must attend consecutively to recover.
  • Track attendance per subject and recalculate weekly.
  • Keep a small margin above the minimum for emergencies.

Frequently asked questions

What is a bunk calculator?
A bunk calculator tells you how many classes you can miss while still staying above a chosen attendance target, or how many you must attend to recover if you have dropped below it.
How many classes can I skip and stay at 75%?
If you have attended A classes out of T held, you can skip roughly floor(A ÷ 0.75 − T) future classes and remain at or above 75%. The calculator above does this instantly.
How do I recover if I am below target?
Attend classes consecutively without missing any. To reach target g you need at least (g × total − attended) ÷ (1 − g) classes in a row. The calculator shows the exact number.
Do cancelled classes affect the calculation?
No. Cancelled classes were never held, so they are not part of the total. Only count classes that actually took place.
