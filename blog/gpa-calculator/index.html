The GPA formula

GPA (Grade Point Average) is a credit-weighted average of your grades. Each grade maps to a number of grade points; each course is worth a number of credit hours.

functions

GPA = Σ (grade points × credit hours) ÷ Σ (credit hours)

Because it is weighted by credits, a high grade in a 4-credit course moves your GPA more than the same grade in a 1-credit course.

GPA vs CGPA

GPA usually describes one semester. CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average) is the running average across every semester you have completed. The formula is identical — CGPA simply includes all your courses to date.

lightbulb

To convert a percentage to CGPA on the 10-point scale, a widely used approximation is CGPA = percentage ÷ 9.5.

What counts as a good GPA?

  • 4.0 scale: 3.5+ is strong, 3.0 is solid, below 2.0 is usually a warning zone.
  • 10-point scale: 8.5+ is excellent, 7.5+ is good.
  • Context matters — scholarships, honours and some employers set their own thresholds.

Key takeaways

  • GPA is a credit-weighted average, not a simple mean of grades.
  • Bigger courses (more credits) affect your GPA more.
  • CGPA is the cumulative version of GPA across all terms.
  • Check your institution's official grade-point chart for exact values.

Frequently asked questions

How is GPA calculated?
GPA is the sum of (grade points × credit hours) for every course, divided by the total credit hours. Courses with more credits weigh more heavily.
What is the difference between GPA and CGPA?
GPA usually refers to a single term, while CGPA (cumulative GPA) is the average across all completed terms. The calculation method is the same.
What is a good GPA?
On a 4.0 scale, 3.5+ is strong and 3.0 is solid. On a 10-point scale, 7.5+ is good. Standards vary by institution and program.
How do I convert percentage to GPA?
A common approximation on the 10-point scale is CGPA = percentage ÷ 9.5. For the 4.0 scale, conversion depends on your school's grade bands, so check its official chart.
